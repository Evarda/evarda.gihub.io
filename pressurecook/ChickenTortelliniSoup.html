Chicken Tortellini Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp. Tomato Paste
  • 4 cups Chicken Stock/Broth
  • 1/4 cup dry White Wine
  • 1 (28 oz.) can Crushed Tomatos (preferably fire roasted)
  • 1 Tbsp. Balsamic Vinegar (preferably aged)
  • 6 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 Tbsp chopped, fresh Oregano or 1.5 Tbsp dried Oregano
  • 2 tsp. Onion Powder
  • 1/2 tsp. Red-Pepper Flakes
  • 1/2 tsp. Salt
  • 1.75-2 lbs. Boneless, Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • 5 oz. Baby Spinach
  • 1 (8-10 oz.) package refridgerated cheese tortellini
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan (for serving)
  • torn/shredded fresh Basil (for serving)
  • Black Pepper (for serving)

Instructions

In a 6- or 8-quart electric pressure cooker, whisk together the tomato paste, stock and wine until the paste dissolves. Stir in the tomatoes, vinegar, garlic, oregano, onion powder, red-pepper flakes and salt. (If you are using unsalted stock, add an additional 1/2 teaspoon salt.) Add the chicken thighs and stir to evenly combine all the ingredients. Close the lid and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes.
Let the pressure release naturally for 15 minutes, then release remaining pressure manually. Open the lid. Turn on the saut√© setting. If you would like a thicker soup (adding Parmesan at the end will thicken the soup as well), remove the chicken with a slotted spoon and allow the soup to bubble until the desired texture is reached, then stir the chicken back in. Stir in the spinach so that it wilts into the soup. Coarsely shred the chicken with two forks, pressing pieces of chicken against the side of the pot to break the thighs apart.
Stir in the tortellini and cook until they are al dente and warmed through, about 3 minutes. Taste the soup and add salt if it tastes flat. Ladle the soup into bowls and top with Parmesan, basil and black pepper.
